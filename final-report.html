SunRoots Dubreka Solar Irrigation Pilot

Print Guide & Electrical Installation Guide

Electrical & Solar PV Components Only

Project Location: Dubreka, Guinea

Coordinates: 9.7898126, -13.4745297

Quick Field Sequence

  1. Confirm panel Voc values and ensure total series Voc stays below 160 V.
  2. Install three identical 500-600 W modules in series and keep orientation strictly South at 10 degrees.
  3. Install one 18-35 A DC breaker between array and controller before commissioning.
  4. Verify clean/dry MC4 connectors, then start and confirm pump operation under sunlight.

1. System Overview

System Description

The irrigation system is powered directly by solar energy without batteries.

Main Components

  • 3 Solar PV Modules (500-600 W each)
  • DC Disconnect Breaker (18-35 A MCB)
  • Ennos 1.5 HP (1.1 kW) DC Sunlight Pump
  • Integrated MPPT Controller
  • Water Pump and Motor

Operating Principle

  1. Sunlight produces electricity from the solar panels.
  2. The three panels supply DC power to the Ennos MPPT controller.
  3. The controller automatically adjusts to obtain maximum power from the solar array.
  4. The controller powers the pump directly.
  5. Pump performance increases with available sunlight.

No batteries are required.

\textbf{Direct-Drive Model} P_{solar}(t) -> MPPT -> P_{pump}(t) \eta_{storage} = 1 \text{ (water elevation instead of batteries)}

2. Solar Panel Selection & Installation

Solar Panel Requirements

Use three identical solar panels rated between 500 W and 600 W.

Critical Voltage Requirement

  • Maximum controller input voltage (Voc): 160 V
  • Each panel MUST have an Open Circuit Voltage (Voc) below 53 V

Example:

  • Panel Voc = 52 V
  • Total series Voc = 52 + 52 + 52 = 156 V Safe

Never exceed 160 V.

For best performance, select panels with a combined series Voc as close as possible to 160 V while remaining below the limit.

\textbf{Voltage Constraint} V_{oc,total} = V_{oc,1} + V_{oc,2} + V_{oc,3} V_{oc,total} < 160\text{V}, \quad V_{oc,panel} < 53\text{V}

Panel Orientation

Direction

  • Face the solar panels strictly South.

This provides the highest annual energy production in Dubreka.

Tilt Angle

Set the panel tilt to: 10 degrees

This corresponds approximately to the local latitude (9.8 degrees) and provides optimal year-round performance.

Shading

Solar panels must receive full sunlight.

Avoid:

  • Trees
  • Bushes
  • Buildings
  • Utility poles
  • Any object creating shadows

Even a small shadow can significantly reduce pump performance.

If vegetation cannot be removed, install longer cables and move the solar array to a better location.

3. Electrical Wiring & Protection

Series Connection

Connect the three panels in series.

Connection order:

Panel 1 (+) -> Panel 2 (-) Panel 2 (+) -> Panel 3 (-) Remaining free positive (+) and free negative (-) go to the DC breaker, then to the Ennos controller.

Series connection increases voltage while maintaining the same current.

\textbf{Series Property} V_{series} = \sum V_i,\quad I_{series} \approx I_{panel}

DC Breaker (Mandatory)

Install one: 18-35 A DC Electrical Breaker (MCB) between the solar array and the pump controller.

Purpose

  • Safe system isolation
  • Protection during maintenance
  • Prevention of short-circuit sparks
  • Protection of the MPPT controller

The system must never be installed without this breaker.

Breaker Location

Install the breaker:

  • As close as possible to the solar panels
  • Inside a weather-protected enclosure

This minimizes overvoltage risks and improves system safety.

Cable Length

If the solar array voltage is close to 150-160 V, limit the cable distance between the panels and the breaker/controller to: Maximum: 15 meters

Longer cables may increase electrical stress and reduce reliability.

MC4 Connector Handling

Always keep MC4 connectors:

  • Clean
  • Dry
  • Free from dust
  • Free from mud

Never leave loose connectors on the ground.

Dirty connectors can:

  • Prevent proper electrical contact
  • Create resistance
  • Cause overheating
  • Produce electrical faults
  • Reduce system performance

4. Operation & Maintenance

Solar Module Cleaning

Inspect regularly.

Remove:

  • Dust
  • Dirt
  • Bird droppings
  • Leaves

Clean using:

  • Soft wet cloth or sponge
  • Clean water

Do not use abrasive materials or harsh chemicals.

Clean modules produce more power.

Vegetation Management

Inspect the area regularly.

Trim:

  • Grass
  • Bushes
  • Tree branches

Prevent any new shading throughout the year.

System Monitoring

The Ennos controller includes:

  • LED display
  • Bluetooth communication

Use the Innovex REMOT / Ennos App to:

  • Check operating status
  • Monitor solar input
  • View pump performance
  • Identify faults
  • Support troubleshooting

Check system status regularly, especially during the dry season.

5. Safety Golden Rules

Rule 1 - Never Exceed 160 V

Before installation:

  • Verify the Voc of each solar panel.
  • Ensure the total series Voc is below 160 V.

Exceeding 160 V can permanently damage the controller.

Rule 2 - Face South at 10 Degrees

Always install the solar array:

  • Facing South
  • Tilted at 10 degrees

Incorrect orientation reduces water production.

Rule 3 - Install the DC Breaker

Every system must include an: 18-35 A DC breaker between the solar panels and the pump controller.

Never bypass or remove this protection device.

Rule 4 - Keep MC4 Connectors Clean

Never place MC4 connectors on:

  • Mud
  • Sand
  • Dust
  • Wet ground

Always inspect and clean connectors before making electrical connections.

Clean connectors ensure reliable operation and prevent electrical faults.

Installation Checklist

  • Three identical 500-600 W solar panels installed
  • Each panel Voc is below 53 V
  • Total series Voc is below 160 V
  • Panels connected in series
  • 18-35 A DC breaker installed near the solar array
  • Cable length less than 15 m when operating near 150-160 V
  • Panels face South
  • Panel tilt set to 10 degrees
  • No shading on the modules
  • MC4 connectors are clean and fully locked
  • LED display and Bluetooth monitoring verified
  • System tested under sunlight and pump operation confirmed
